The default color picker in most design tools is fine for basics, but professional designers need more control. That's why we built the **Advanced Color Picker V2.0**. It's not just about selecting a color; it's about understanding how that color fits into your broader design system.

## Key Features

### 1. Multi-Model Support
Switch seamlessly between color models depending on your needs:
*   **HEX:** For web development.
*   **RGB:** For screen-based design.
*   **HSL:** For intuitive adjustments (Hue, Saturation, Lightness).
*   **CMYK:** For print compatibility checks.

### 2. Automatic Harmonies
Stop guessing which colors go together. As you pick a base color, our engine automatically generates six distinct harmony schemes:
*   **Complementary**
*   **Analogous**
*   **Triadic**
*   **Split Complementary**
*   **Tetradic**
*   **Monochromatic**

Clicking any of these generated colors instantly sets it as your active color, allowing you to explore tangents and variations endlessly.

### 3. Real-Time Contrast Checking
Accessibility is built-in, not an afterthought. The picker displays the **WCAG Contrast Ratio** against white in real-time.
*   **Green Badge (Pass):** Contrast is ≥ 4.5:1 (AA Standard).
*   **Red Badge (Fail):** Contrast is < 4.5:1.

## Why HSL is Better for UI

If you're still tweaking colors using an RGB slider or a simple HEX picker, you're doing it the hard way. **HSL (Hue, Saturation, Lightness)** is the most human-readable format.

*   **Want it brighter?** Increase Lightness.
*   **Want it more vibrant?** Increase Saturation.
*   **Want a different color but same intensity?** Shift the Hue.

This makes creating **Monochromatic Scales** for your UI (e.g., Primary-100 to Primary-900) incredibly easy. Just lock the Hue and Saturation, and step down the Lightness by 10% increments.

## History & Favorites

Design exploration is messy. You find a great color, tweak it, and then realize the previous version was better.
*   **History:** We remember your last 20 picks automatically.
*   **Favorites:** Click the heart icon to save a color permanently to your local library.
